Factors for Human longevity

(An In-Depth Visualization that reveals factors for Prolonged Living)

Over the year, the life expectancy of a human has been increasing at a steady pace. From the early year of 2000 at 67 years old to the year 2015 at 72 years old which mean that human on average is living 5 years longer. This might be due to access to education, better access to medical technology, better socioeconomic situation for the induvial, and an overall richer quality of life to its prior decade.

The following visualisation present data which are collected over the past decade detailing each country life expectancy, economy, percentage of vaccination against disease, and the overall wellbeing of the country. This visualisation hope to find a certain correlation between certain factors and hope to conclude which factor would lead to human life longetivity.

World Map

The map visualisation displays the average life expectancy in a country on that particular year. The colour Saturation on each country shows how much the average life expectancy is for its populace and the darker it is the higher the life expectancy. Beneath the map there is a slider to show the change of life expectancy for each country over time.

On average, those that lives on the continent of North America, Australia, and Europe has a much deeper saturation of colour than other countries. This is because these categories of continent are considered a developed nation. As Miladinov (2020) stated, the increased economic development, higher living standard, and improved health is still a relevant factor for a prolonged life. Due to that most developed nation on average would have higher life expectancy due to this factor.

However, there are some anomaly such as Chille where it has the life expectancy usually found in a developing nation. Due to this we would look in-depth on what the difference is between developed nation and developing nation to find out more factor that affect the life expectancy of each country

developing VS developed country

As the visualisation discussed above, the difference between a developing country and a developed countries life expectancies stems from many factor. factor such as economic development, higher living standard and improved health, due to that this section wishes to see the difference more in depth to see the difference between the two statuses.
